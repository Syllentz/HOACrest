1 Governing Documents

Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions (DCCR)

The master governing document for Mason Woods — the community's "constitution." Establishes all rules that bind every current and future homeowner. Covers property rights, assessments, architectural controls, common area rules, and enforcement powers. Recorded under Harris County Clerk's File No. RP-2022-378389.

Key Facts
  • Defines all property use restrictions — what you can and can't do with your lot and home
  • Establishes mandatory annual assessments (HOA dues) for community maintenance
  • Creates the Architectural Review Committee (ARC) — all exterior changes need approval before work begins
  • Gives the Association power to enforce rules through fines, liens, and legal action
  • Defines common areas, reserves, easements, and who maintains what
  • Sets rules for leasing/renting your property
  • Establishes the Association's authority to adopt additional policies and guidelines
  • All owners and future owners are bound — these covenants "run with the land"

Detailed Breakdown

Overview

The Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions (DCCR) is the foundational legal document for the Mason Woods Community Association. It was recorded with the Harris County Clerk and applies to every lot within the community. Every homeowner, and every future purchaser of property in Mason Woods, is bound by this document.

Property Use Restrictions

  • Lots are restricted to single-family residential use unless otherwise specified.
  • No business, commercial, or professional activity may be conducted on any lot that is visible from the exterior or generates traffic, noise, or other nuisances.
  • Vehicles must be parked in garages or driveways. No inoperable vehicles, boats, trailers, or recreational vehicles may be stored in view.
  • Temporary structures, including storage containers and portable buildings, are not permitted without Association approval.

Assessments

  • The Association levies annual assessments on every lot to fund community maintenance, insurance, reserves, and operations.
  • Special assessments may be levied for capital improvements or unexpected expenses with proper Board approval.
  • Unpaid assessments accrue interest and late fees and may result in a lien on the property.

Architectural Review

  • The Architectural Review Committee (ARC) must approve all exterior modifications before work begins.
  • Applications must include plans, materials, colors, and a site plan showing the proposed changes.
  • The ARC has the authority to deny applications that do not conform to community standards.

Common Areas & Maintenance

  • The Association owns and maintains common areas including parks, pools, detention areas, and landscape medians.
  • Individual lot owners are responsible for maintaining their own lots, including landscaping, fences, and structural elements.
  • Easements for drainage, utilities, and access are defined and must remain unobstructed.

Enforcement

  • The Association may enforce the DCCR through fines, suspension of common area privileges, liens, and legal action.
  • Owners are entitled to notice and an opportunity for a hearing before fines are assessed.
  • The Board may adopt additional rules and policies to supplement the DCCR.

Leasing

  • Owners may lease their property subject to Association approval and specific leasing restrictions.
  • Tenants are bound by all community rules and the owner remains responsible for compliance.

Board of Directors

  • The Board governs the Association's business and affairs.
  • Directors are elected at the annual meeting by the membership.
  • Term lengths, staggered terms, and vacancy procedures are specified.
  • Directors must be members in good standing (assessments current).

Meetings

  • Annual meetings are held at a date, time, and place set by the Board with proper advance notice to all members.
  • Special meetings may be called by the Board President, a majority of the Board, or upon written request of a specified percentage of members.
  • Notice requirements specify minimum days of advance written notice for annual and special meetings.
  • A quorum must be present (in person or by proxy) for votes to be valid.

Voting

  • Each lot has one vote.
  • Proxy voting is permitted under specific rules — proxies must be in writing.
  • Certain major decisions (e.g., amendments, special assessments) may require a supermajority.

Officers

  • President: presides over meetings, executes contracts, and is the primary spokesperson.
  • Vice President: acts in the President's absence.
  • Secretary: maintains records, minutes, and official correspondence.
  • Treasurer: oversees financial records, budgets, and assessment collections.

Committees

  • The Board may create committees (including the ARC) and delegate specific authority.
  • Committee members are appointed by the Board and serve at the Board's discretion.

Fiscal Management

  • The Board prepares and adopts an annual budget.
  • Financial records are maintained and available for member inspection per the Access policy.
  • Reserve funds are maintained for major repairs and replacements.

Purpose

Each Supplemental Amendment annexes a specific section of land into the Mason Woods Community Association and subjects it to the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions. The amendments for Sections 1, 4, 7, and 8 each follow a similar structure with section-specific environmental disclosures.

Minimum Living Area

  • All homes must contain a minimum of 1,200 square feet of air-conditioned living area, exclusive of garages, porches, and patios.

Environmental Conditions & Restricted Reserves

  • Restricted reserves include detention ponds, drainage channels, and environmentally sensitive areas that may be adjacent to residential lots.
  • Detention reserves are designed to manage stormwater and may include decorative fountains maintained by the Association.
  • Gas transmission easements cross certain lots. Construction on these easements is strictly prohibited.
  • Land adjacent to the community that is not owned by the Association may be developed or change use at any time without notice to homeowners.

Wildlife & Natural Conditions

  • Wildlife including snakes, insects, coyotes, and other animals may be present in or near restricted reserves and detention areas.
  • The Association does not control or manage wildlife populations in these areas.
  • Owners are advised to exercise caution, especially near reserve boundaries.

Liability & Indemnification

  • Each lot owner assumes all liability for environmental conditions on or affecting their lot.
  • Owners fully indemnify the Association, the Declarant, and their agents from any environmental claims.
  • Lot owners may not alter drainage patterns or interfere with the natural or engineered flow of stormwater.

Solar Energy Devices

This policy governs the installation and placement of solar energy devices on individual lots within Mason Woods, pursuant to Texas Property Code Sections 202.010 and 202.011.

  • Solar energy devices may be installed on the roof of the home or within a fenced yard or patio owned by the homeowner.
  • Panels cannot extend higher than the roofline or beyond the edge of the roof.
  • Preferred placement is on the back of the home. Alternate locations are only permitted if they would produce at least 10% more energy, as determined using the NREL PVWatts modeling tool.
  • The top edge of panels must be parallel to the roofline and conform to the roof slope.
  • All visible frames, brackets, and wiring must be silver, bronze, or black.
  • Yard-mounted devices within a fenced area cannot be taller than the fence.
  • Installation must not void any material warranties on the home.
  • ARC approval is required before installation begins.
  • Solar devices may not be installed on Association or common property.
  • The Association generally cannot withhold approval if the device meets all requirements, unless the placement would cause unreasonable discomfort or annoyance to other residents.

Roofing Materials

  • Permitted materials include: wind/hail resistant products, materials with superior heating and cooling efficiency compared to standard composite, and solar-generation capable roofing materials.
  • Any replacement roofing must resemble the authorized subdivision shingles in appearance, be of equal or better quality, and match the aesthetic of surrounding homes.
  • ARC application required: submit a completed application with a site or roof plan, current photos, manufacturer brochures, and material samples.

Overview

This policy regulates the installation of swimming pool and spa safety enclosure fences on residential lots in Mason Woods, pursuant to Texas Property Code Section 202.022.

Definition

  • A swimming pool enclosure is a fence surrounding a pool or spa that uses transparent mesh or clear panels in metal frames, is a maximum of 6 feet tall, and is designed to be non-climbable.

Pre-Approved Enclosures

  • Black transparent mesh in metal frames, 6 feet or under, that conform to applicable safety codes are pre-approved and do not require an ARC application.

Other Enclosures

  • Any enclosure that does not meet the pre-approved specifications requires ARC approval before installation.
  • Non-standard enclosures are limited to 6 feet in height unless the ARC specifically approves a taller enclosure.
  • Frame materials: metal, wood, or polycarbonate plastic.
  • Frame colors: white, silver, transparent, or black tones.
  • Panel materials: transparent mesh, glass, or polycarbonate plastic.
  • Panel colors: clear, white, or light blue.

ARC Application

  • Application must include a site plan, type/description of enclosure, and manufacturer brochures or material samples.
  • The ARC considers: pool size, lot configuration, lot location, and visibility from streets, neighboring lots, and common areas.

Compliance

  • Non-compliance constitutes a violation of the Dedicatory Instruments and may result in enforcement action.

  • Owners must request a hearing in writing within 30 days of the Final Notice mailing date.
  • The hearing is held within 30 days of the request. One postponement of up to 10 days is allowed.
  • The Board provides at least 10 days' advance notice of the hearing date, time, and place, along with a Hearing Packet containing all relevant documents and photographs.
  • The owner may present evidence and must submit documentary evidence at least 5 days before the hearing.
  • Both sides may audio record the hearing.
  • The Board issues its ruling within 30 days. If no ruling is communicated, the original determination stands.
  • One hearing per Final Notice, unless the Board grants an additional hearing.
  • Alternative dispute resolution is available per Texas Property Code Section 209.007(e).

Attorney Referral & Foreclosure

  • The Association's attorney may send demand letters, file lawsuits, institute expedited foreclosure, or file bankruptcy motions.
  • Foreclosure requires a 61-day notice to lien holders per Texas Property Code Sections 209.0091 and 209.0092.
  • NSF or returned check fees equal the bank's charges plus the Association's administrative costs.

Payment Plans

  • Minimum term: 3 months (the Board determines the actual term).
  • Equal monthly installments, due on the 1st of each month.
  • Missing the first payment constitutes default on the entire plan.
  • Owners may request a longer payment period in writing.
  • The Association is not required to offer a payment plan to an owner who defaulted on a previous plan within the last 2 years.
  • If the plan extends into the next assessment cycle, current assessments must also be paid on time.
  • No obligation to offer a plan after the 30-day cure period expires.
  • Maximum one payment plan per 12-month period.

Payment Priority

  • Payments are applied in the following order: delinquent assessments, current assessments, attorney fees related to foreclosure, other attorney fees, late fees, fines, and other amounts.
